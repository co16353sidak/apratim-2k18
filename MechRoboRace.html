Robo Race

Robo Race

Design and fabricate an autonomous or manual vehicle capable of navigating efficiently through all possible terrains like sand, pebbles, obstacles, bumpers, bridges, marbles, grease, inclines, dips, cliffs, ramps, banks, etc. The vehicle must be able to effectively follow the path. The robots will be tested on speed and how well it navigates through the defined path.

Robo Race

Robot Specifications

  1. The maximum dimension of the robot can be 30 cm x 30 cm (l x b).
  2. The robot may be wired or wireless.
  3. The length of the wire (for wired bots) should be long enough to cover the whole track and wire should remain slack during the complete run.
  4. Max weight must not exceed 6 kg.
  5. The power supply can be of maximum 24Volt.
  6. Readymade toys car are not allowed.
  7. Battery may be on-board or off-board.

Rules

  1. Robot should be as per the given specifications.
  2. Each team can have maximum 3 members. Students from different institutes can form a team.
  3. No entry fee.
  4. Each member of the team must contain the identity card of his/her respected institute.
  5. The robot should not damage the track.
  6. The robot must not leave behind any of its parts during the run; else it will result in disqualification.
  7. Unethical behaviour could lead to disqualification.
  8. Faculty-coordinators have all the rights to take final decision for any matter during the event.
  9. Certificates will be given to all the participants.
  10. The track would be constructed on ground and multiple obstacles (which will be revealed at the time of the event) will span the track.

The competition will be carried out in two rounds:


Round 1: Eliminations


  • The task is to reach the finish line in least amount of time.
  • Time penalties for line touching and skipping an obstacle will be given.
  • Two heats will be given to the participants and the better of the two will be considered as score.
  • Top 5 teams will be eligible for finals.

Round 2: Finals


  • The task is the same as the first round.
  • Single heat will be given and it will be considered as round 2 score.
  • Time penalties may be different for this round.
  • The Team with highest score in round 2 will be declared as the WINNER.

Decision of the organising team will be final and no challenge shall be accepted once the final decision is made.
